**Sodium:** High intake can lead to high blood pressure, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke.

**Total Carbohydrate:** High intake can lead to weight gain and increased risk of type 2 diabetes.

**Total Sugars:** High intake can lead to weight gain, tooth decay, and increased risk of type 2 diabetes.

**Dietary Fiber:** Low intake can lead to constipation, hemorrhoids, and diverticular disease.

**Vitamin C:** Low intake can lead to scurvy, a disease that causes bleeding gums, bruising, and impaired wound healing.

**Calcium:** Low intake can lead to osteoporosis, a condition that weakens bones and makes them more susceptible to fracture.

**Iron:** Low intake can lead to anemia, a condition that causes fatigue, weakness, and shortness of breath.
